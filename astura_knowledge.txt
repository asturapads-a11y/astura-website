
This knowledge base provides educational information on menstrual health and hygiene. The content is intended for general informational purposes only and should not replace professional medical advice. If you have serious symptoms or concerns, please consult a healthcare provider.

# Menstruation basics
Menstruation, or having a period, is a normal biological process during which the lining of the uterus is shed through the vaginahttps://www.cdc.gov/hygiene/about/menstrual-hygiene.html#:~:text=Menstruation%20%28also%20called%20a%20,the%20body%20through%20the%20vagina. A typical menstrual cycle occurs every 23–35 days and bleeding usually lasts 2–7 dayshttps://www.nhs.uk/conditions/periods/starting-periods/#:~:text=Why%20have%20my%20periods%20not,started%20yet.

# Menstrual hygiene tips
Proper hygiene during your period helps prevent infections, reduces odours and keeps you comfortablehttps://www.cdc.gov/hygiene/about/menstrual-hygiene.html#:~:text=Key%20points. Key recommendations include:
- **Change products regularly:** Change sanitary pads every few hours and tampons every 4–8 hourshttps://www.cdc.gov/hygiene/about/menstrual-hygiene.html#:~:text=,8%20hours%20at%20a%20time. Do not wear a single tampon for more than 8 hourshttps://www.cdc.gov/hygiene/about/menstrual-hygiene.html#:~:text=,absorbency%20may%20be%20too%20high.
- **Keep the genital area clean:** Wash the external genital area (vulva) daily with water and avoid scented productshttps://www.cdc.gov/hygiene/about/menstrual-hygiene.html#:~:text=,yeast%20infection%20or%20bacterial%20vaginosis.
- **Use unscented products:** Choose unscented pads, tampons or toilet paper to avoid irritationhttps://www.cdc.gov/hygiene/about/menstrual-hygiene.html#:~:text=,impact%20your%20natural%20pH%20balance.
- **Discard products properly:** Wrap used disposable products and place them in a trash bin; do not flush them down the toilethttps://www.cdc.gov/hygiene/about/menstrual-hygiene.html#:~:text=,menstrual%20products%20down%20the%20toilet.
- **Wear breathable clothing:** Lightweight, breathable underwear helps keep the area dry and reduces the risk of infectionhttps://www.cdc.gov/hygiene/about/menstrual-hygiene.html#:~:text=,Trapped%20moisture%20provides%20a.
- **Stay hydrated and track your cycle:** Drinking plenty of liquids and monitoring your cycle can help identify unusual changeshttps://www.cdc.gov/hygiene/about/menstrual-hygiene.html#:~:text=,phone%20designed%20for%20this%20purpose.

# Age of the first period (menarche)
Most girls experience their first period (menarche) between ages 10 and 15, with an average around 12 yearshttps://www.uchicagomedicine.org/forefront/pediatrics-articles/getting-your-period-normal-menstrual-cycle-teens-preteens#:~:text=Most%20adolescents%C2%A0get%20their%20first%20period%C2%A0between,is%20not%20always%20the%20casehttps://www.nhs.uk/conditions/periods/starting-periods/#:~:text=Most%20girls%20start%20their%20periods,to%20make%20sure%20they%27re%20prepared. Menarche often occurs about 2 to 3 years after breasts begin to develophttps://www.uchicagomedicine.org/forefront/pediatrics-articles/getting-your-period-normal-menstrual-cycle-teens-preteens#:~:text=Children%20usually%20have%20their%20first,be%20experiencing%20their%20first%20period. It is still normal to start as early as 8 or 9 or as late as 15https://www.nhs.uk/conditions/periods/starting-periods/#:~:text=Most%20girls%20start%20their%20periods,to%20make%20sure%20they%27re%20preparedhttps://my.clevelandclinic.org/health/diseases/24139-menarche#:~:text=You%E2%80%99ll%20likely%20get%20your%20period,your%20breasts%20begin%20to%20develop. Consult a healthcare provider if periods have not started by age 15 or if there are no signs of puberty (such as breast development) by age 13https://www.nhs.uk/conditions/periods/starting-periods/#:~:text=Why%20have%20my%20periods%20not,started%20yet.

# Normal variation and common symptoms
It is common for cycles to be irregular in the first few years after menarchehttps://www.uchicagomedicine.org/forefront/pediatrics-articles/getting-your-period-normal-menstrual-cycle-teens-preteens#:~:text=Often%2C%20when%20a%20person%20begins,and%20symptoms%20become%20more%20predictable. Periods may start earlier or later each month and vary in length or heaviness. Typical premenstrual and menstrual symptoms can include cramping, bloating, mood changes, acne breakouts, and breast tendernesshttps://www.uchicagomedicine.org/forefront/pediatrics-articles/getting-your-period-normal-menstrual-cycle-teens-preteens#:~:text=,such%20as%20bloating%20or%20diarrheahttps://my.clevelandclinic.org/health/diseases/24139-menarche#:~:text=Leading%20up%20to%20your%20first,you%20may%20notice%20these%20symptoms. Everyone experiences periods differently; do not worry if your symptoms differ from othershttps://my.clevelandclinic.org/health/diseases/24139-menarche#:~:text=Everyone%20experiences%20periods%20differently,are%20different%20from%20someone%20else%E2%80%99s.

# Signs of heavy menstrual bleeding and bleeding disorders
Heavy menstrual bleeding may be a sign of a bleeding disorder. Warning signs include:
- Bleeding that lasts longer than seven dayshttps://www.cdc.gov/female-blood-disorders/signs-symptoms/index.html#:~:text=Heavy%20bleeding%20during%20menstruation%20,that%20can%20include.
- Soaking through a tampon or pad every hour or experiencing blood gushing that limits daily activitieshttps://www.cdc.gov/female-blood-disorders/signs-symptoms/index.html#:~:text=Heavy%20bleeding%20during%20menstruation%20,that%20can%20include.
- Passing clots larger than a grapehttps://www.cdc.gov/female-blood-disorders/signs-symptoms/index.html#:~:text=Heavy%20bleeding%20during%20menstruation%20,that%20can%20include.
- Receiving treatment for anaemia or being diagnosed as “low in iron”https://www.cdc.gov/female-blood-disorders/signs-symptoms/index.html#:~:text=Heavy%20bleeding%20during%20menstruation%20,that%20can%20include.
Other symptoms of a potential bleeding disorder may include frequent nosebleeds lasting more than ten minutes, easy bruising without injury, excessive bleeding after medical procedures, or a family history of bleeding disordershttps://www.cdc.gov/female-blood-disorders/signs-symptoms/index.html#:~:text=Heavy%20bleeding%20during%20menstruation%20,that%20can%20include. If you experience any of these symptoms, contact a healthcare professionalhttps://www.cdc.gov/female-blood-disorders/signs-symptoms/index.html#:~:text=When%20to%20talk%20to%20your,doctor.

# When to seek medical care
You should talk to a doctor or healthcare professional if:
- Your periods have not started by age 15 or you have no signs of puberty by age 13https://www.nhs.uk/conditions/periods/starting-periods/#:~:text=Why%20have%20my%20periods%20not,started%20yet.
- You experience changes in vaginal odour, extreme or unusual pain, or a heavier flow or longer period than usualhttps://www.cdc.gov/hygiene/about/menstrual-hygiene.html.
- You are concerned about heavy bleeding, especially if periods last longer than seven days or interfere with daily activitieshttps://www.cdc.gov/female-blood-disorders/signs-symptoms/index.html#:~:text=Heavy%20bleeding%20during%20menstruation%20,that%20can%20include.
- You have irregular cycles that do not stabilise after a few years of menstruationhttps://www.uchicagomedicine.org/forefront/pediatrics-articles/getting-your-period-normal-menstrual-cycle-teens-preteens#:~:text=Often%2C%20when%20a%20person%20begins,and%20symptoms%20become%20more%20predictable.
- You have other symptoms such as severe cramps, fainting, dizziness, or unusual discharge. Seek immediate medical attention if you suspect you may be pregnant and experience heavy bleeding or severe abdominal pain.

# Contraception and fertility awareness
Remember that menstruation signifies that pregnancy is possible if you have vaginal intercourse without contraceptionhttps://my.clevelandclinic.org/health/diseases/24139-menarche#:~:text=You%E2%80%99ll%20likely%20get%20your%20period,your%20breasts%20begin%20to%20develop. If you want to avoid pregnancy or understand your cycle better, talk to a healthcare provider about family planning and contraception options.
